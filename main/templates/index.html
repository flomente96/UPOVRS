GUIDELINES

1. For use of classrooms for the sole purpose of holding meeting of UP student organization from 8:00am-5:30pm, approval will be at the OSA level only. Announcements can also be apporved right away by OSA (particularly in charge for student organizations) for posting upon coordination

2. Reservation have to be field at least three (3) days in advance for UP approved activiteis and the least one (1) week for Non-UP activities. In any case Nuit-wide activities will supersede any prior reservation.

3. Reservations with CDMO can be made in advance, but it can only be considered a valid reservation if activity has been approved and form has been accomplished.

4. A maximum of one (1) week is the allowable period for reservations of Conference Hall/AVR subject to renewal if activity is beyond one(1) week. Long term reservations are not allowed.

5. Reservation is on a first come first served basis. (In case of conflict, the one who filed late has to request for reconsideration from the one who filed earlier.)

6. Activities beyond 5:30PM on weekdays and Saturdays, Sundays and Holidays are subject to the approval of the Chancellor.

7. Activities which will disturb classes and offices shall not be allowed.

8. Eating and drinking inside the Conferense Hall/AVR are strictly prohibited.

9. The Conference Hall/AVR can not be used for any activity involving children.

10. Officers and the sponsoring organization shall be held responsible for any damages or loss of property, noted during and immediately after the activity.

11. For income generating activities, the sponsoring organization shall pay for the use of aircon, electricity, among others (consult the Accounting Office for computation of rates or see rates page)

12. Honoraria/overtime for the technician, utility personnel rendering services after office hours will be paid by the sponsoring organization/officers.

13. For Arts and Science/Undergraduate Lobbies:

- The from should be routed first to: (1) PE teachers, (2) Room USe Committee Chair and (3) Office of Students Affairs for the purpose of coordinating activities so as not ro disrupt classes in the Arts & Sciences and the Undergraduate buildings.

14. For multimedia projector (MMP)/LCD rental charges:

- The actual nubmer of hours will be noted in #12 of the form by ARV/CH In-charge for billing at the Cahsier's office after the activity. Upon payment, the organizer shall present the official receipt to the AVR/CH In-charge for clearance.

Note: Public renters/Outisde organizations partnered with a UP-based organization shall post a P5,000.00 bond for the use of Performing Arts Hall and AVR1 (library building), to be paid at the cashier (for deposit to a trust fund) upon approval of the permit to use to answer for the damages/losses of the rented hall/room. After the activity, RENTER submits a request for return of the bond to the ADA with supporting documents: clearance from CDMO and official receipt of the posted bond.
